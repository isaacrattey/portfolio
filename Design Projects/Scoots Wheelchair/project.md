A customer approached us to ask if we could design a wheelchair for the puppy she was fostering, named Scoot, who didn’t have functioning front legs. The timeline was tight because Scoot was growing so quickly, and getting accustomed to “scooting” across the floor rather than walking (not that she had much of a choice). This project was challenging because front-wheelchairs are much less common than rear-wheelchairs for dogs, and they need to be much more customized to the needs of the specific animal.

As I started designing, my goals were to make the wheelchair as customizable as possible while keeping it inexpensive. We were funding the project ourselves, and we didn’t want to invest a ton of money into a wheelchair that Scoot would outgrow after a few weeks. My first design had three omniwheels since I thought this would give her good maneuverability and help her balance - like a three legged stool. I found this design was too prone to tipping, and the omniwheels were too difficult for her to control. This design was also mostly 3D printed which made it fragile. During the testing for that design, I learned a lot about how difficult the wheelchair would be for Scoot to push. My second design used 4 casters, and a frame made out of bent conduit. This worked better, but Scoot had issues with drifting and keeping positioned in the center of the wheelchair. With this design, I learned that having a handle for guiding Scoot was very helpful for building her confidence and control.

The final design used two wheels with a bent conduit frame, like the previous design. It allowed her to practice walking with her owner helping guide her, and she was able to maneuver and get around much easier.
