### Overview
Design Climate was a Duke course focused on creating solutions to urgent environmental challenges. The course partnered with DesignHub to make these solutions into functional prototypes, and the Wave Trough was the first of these projects. The Wave Trough demonstrated how seagrass beds could reduce the intensity of ocean waves and reduce shoreline erosion by creating and measuring waves when the trough was filled with water. A waterproof servo moved a paddle back and forth under the water, creating a wave that induced microcurrents in an electromagnet, which were displayed on a galvanometer. When seagrass was added or removed from the trough, viewers could see the difference in the size of the waves produced by looking at the galvanometer readings.

The project was featured in a WRAL news article: [Duke University students showcase climate solutions at Energy Week event](https://www.wral.com/story/duke-university-students-showcase-climate-solutions-at-energy-week-event/21722662/).

Co-Lab blog post: [Design Hub and Design Climate collaborate on wave tank project](https://colab.duke.edu/blog-post/design-hub-and-design-climate-collaborate-wave-tank-project/).
